Research comes in many forms. In my line of work, research means something very specific. Research is direct, intentional, necessary, and something that is consumable, producable. This type of research, for me, requires diliberate effort. Computer science comes more naturally. I find computer logic and programming very entertaining. But the aspects of research that latch onto my brain most readily are the topics of the void. The void is a zone of unfettered fascination. The topics there occupy significant mental reserves for weeks or months at a time.

I believe that we owe it to ourselves as agents of (perceived) choice to maximize our engagement with topics and experiences that interest us. To deny yourself a space to be fascinated and elevated by the rush of learning something new is to deny yourself life. Learn something new about your mind, about the world, about what you're capable of.
