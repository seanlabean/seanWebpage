Asides in the Life of Code

My style of engagement with programming projects has always troubled me, but I believe I am beginning to accept and revel in my passion. I learn in spurts and fits. I will periodically pick up an interest in a programming regime and consume as much content as I can on it as quickly as possible. This in turn fires up my passion and drive to engage with the topic further, I'll start a project, start learning, start day dreaming of a an incredible breakthrough a new level of understanding that will radically change my life.

But then I'll get bored. And the work reveals itself to be much harder than I could have imagined. And I'll get discouraged. And I'll discover something else interesting.

I have spent a long time chasing my tail with areas of interest like this. I never feel like a finish a project or take it to the level of making money or encountering something profound. So eventually I decided: screw it, I'll embrace what fleeting interest I have and pull together a million half-assed passion projects. These will be my legacy, my foundational knowledge and experience upon which I will build my career. No need to bother myself with excruciating details and methods and lessons I dont understand, if a topic is truly interesting to me, I will naturally push my limits. Otherwise, why bother, this is just for fun anyways.

This philosophy has worked very well for me lately and inspired the creation of my ATAVATA persona: a digital manifestation of this malleable interest set of mine. Showcasing these projects to other people has helped me realize a lot of these are pretty damn cool and impressive, something to be proud of.
